## Stablecoin Routing
Routes stablecoins (e.g., USDC, USDT) across major networks (Ethereum, Base, Arbitrum, etc.) while maintaining a canonical supply:

- **Canonical Supply** - no wrapped or synthetic representations.
- **Adapter-Based Routing** - integrates with pluggable adapters (e.g., CCTPv2 etc.,) to enable issuer verified cross-chain transfers.
- **Deterministic Settlement** - tokens are burned on the source chain and minted on the destination upon verified authorization.
- **Settlement Finality** - mints require signed attestations from the issuer verifiable on-chain.

This design ensures liquidity is **fungible, auditable, and settlement-grade** - essential for institutional adoption.

## Canonical Stability Pool
The **Stability Pool** is Surgeâ€™s liquidation clearinghouse. While stablecoin activity spans multiple chains, all liquidation flows resolve in the **single canonical pool**.

- **Implementation** - a stateful contract deployed on a chosen execution chain (e.g., Ethereum).
- **Inputs** - stablecoins deposited by liquidity providers.
- **Outputs** - discounted BTC distributed to pool participants during liquidations.
- **Market Integrity** - Centralizes bad debt absorption, preventing fragmented liquidations or MEV-driven arbitrage.
